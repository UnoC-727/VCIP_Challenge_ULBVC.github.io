Ultra Low-Bitrate Video Compression Challenge

Focusing on innovative, perceptually-driven, low-latency video coding.
Tracks: 50kbps & 200kbps

About the Challenge

The VCIP 2025 Low-Bitrate Video Compression Challenge aims to advance video compression technology for extremely limited bandwidth scenarios, with a particular focus on ultra low-bitrates (50kbps and 200kbps) where existing methods suffer significant degradation in quality and efficiency. While mainstream streaming services operate at higher bitrates, real-world applications such as satellite internet (e.g., Starlink) often face severe bandwidth restrictions, resulting in poor video quality. Traditional compression benchmarks have largely emphasized objective metrics at conventional bitrates, leaving a critical gap in research targeting ultra low-bitrate environments and perceptual quality assessment. To address this gap, the VCIP 2025 Low-Bitrate Video Compression Challenge explicitly emphasizes both extreme bitrate constraints and subjective visual quality evaluation, encouraging innovative AI-powered and perceptually optimized codecs. By incorporating both objective and subjective assessments, the competition aims to stimulate the development of next-generation video codecs that can deliver high perceptual quality in low-latency, bandwidth-constrained scenarios. The challenge further encourages the exploration of generative AI and foundational models, reflecting recent advances in video diffusions, text-to-video and video synthesis, to fundamentally improve compression efficiency and perceptual quality at ultra low bitrates.

Tracks & Requirements

50kbps Track (Low-bitrate)

  • Total decoding complexity:
    Average ≤ 1000 kMac/pixel
  • Maximum decoding time:
    7000 ms

200kbps Track (High-bitrate)

  • Total decoding complexity:
    Average ≤ 300 kMac/pixel
  • Maximum decoding time:
    125 ms (approximately 8fps)

Note: All complexity constraints are evaluated based on the average computational cost per pixel for 720P resolution video.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Preliminary Evaluation: Submissions will be ranked using objective metrics, including PSNR, VMAF, and LPIPS.
  • Final Evaluation: Winners will be selected through a human subjective evaluation task. Raters will compare the compressed outputs to the original videos and select their preferred codec based on perceived quality.
  • Computational Complexity: The total average per-pixel computation for each track must meet the specified requirements, as measured on 720P videos.
  • Bitrate Constraint: The average must remain below 50/200kbps, as applicable for each track.
